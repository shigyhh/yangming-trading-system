I.The Origin

Five centuries ago,
a philosopher told his students:
don't sit on cushions.
Polish yourself on real affairs.

Wang Yangming (1472–1529) founded the 心学 — the School of Mind — at a time when most Eastern philosophy lived in monasteries. He insisted on the opposite: that the self can only be refined through real engagement with the world. He called this 事上磨练 — polishing on affairs.

Wang Yangming was no recluse. He was a general, a governor, a man who quelled rebellions while writing letters about how to extend the innate knowing of the heart. His students didn't meditate their way to wisdom. They did things, and let the doing teach them.

"The market is the most demanding affair of our time."— A modern reading of 事上磨练

Trading is not a problem of information. It is a problem of self. Wang Yangming saw this five hundred years before Bloomberg terminals existed, before crypto, before the chart on your phone. His school is built precisely for the person who keeps making the same mistake — and knows it.

ZHIXING is that school, redrawn for the trader.

II.The Gap

Your strategy
was trained.
You weren't.

What you learned

You studied charts. You learned position sizing. You read every book on technical analysis. You back-tested. You journaled. You built a system that, on paper, works.

And still — at the moment of execution — something else takes over.

What still happens

You chase a top you swore you wouldn't. You hold a loser past your stop. You revenge-trade twenty minutes after a defeat. You watch yourself do these things, and the watching does not stop them.

This is not a flaw in your strategy. It is the untrained one inside you. ZHIXING is the practice that trains the one inside.

III.The Path

Six Gates. 六关.
A complete path.

From the first vow you make as a trader, to the lifelong cultivation that mature traders return to daily. Each gate is a discrete practice with its own training, its own measurement, and its own seal.

志
I.

Setting the Will

Lìzhì
立志

The trader's covenant. Before the market is permitted to test you, your rules must already exist — written, signed, and immovable.

Training
  • No stop, no entry.
  • Position size precedes the trade.
  • The plan is older than the impulse.
照
II.

Illuminating the Heart

Zhàoxīn
照心

See the affliction before it moves your hand. Five recur in every trader. Naming them is the first form of mastery.

The Five Afflictions
  • Greed · 贪
  • Fear · 惧
  • Haste · 急
  • Anger · 怒
  • Fatigue · 疲
磨
III.

Polishing on Affairs

Shì shàng mó
事上磨

Where most strategies die. The classroom is the chart in motion. Bar-by-bar, decision-by-decision, the gap between plan and act narrows.

Training
  • Cutting losses on time.
  • Not chasing rallies.
  • Not holding losers.
破
IV.

Breaking the Mind Thief

Pò xīnzéi
破心贼

"To defeat the bandits in the mountains is easy. To defeat the thieves in the mind is hard." — Wang Yangming

Each trader carries one dominant pattern. Identify it. Train against it. Dissolve it.

The Three Thieves
  • The Gambler · 赌徒
  • The Impulsive · 冲动
  • The Hostage · 扛单
合
V.

Unity of Knowing & Doing

Zhīxíng héyī
知行合一

The signature teaching of the school, and the signature metric of this practice. When the distance between your plan and your action approaches zero, you have arrived here.

Measurement
  • Streak of clean execution.
  • No plan changes mid-trade.
  • The ZHIXING Index ≥ 80, sustained.
良
VI.

Extending the Innate Knowing

Zhì liángzhī
致良知

Not a final destination. An infinite practice. The mature trader does not graduate from this gate — they return to it daily, lifelong, advancing only in degree.

Lifelong
  • Self-led reflection.
  • Awareness without correction.
  • The practice becomes the person.

A gate is not a level. A gate is a door you keep walking through.

IV.The Instruments

Three instruments
of the practice.

I.

Daily Heart Witness

心证

Three hundred and sixty-five days of structured cultivation. One precept, one witness, one training, one reflection — for each day of the year. A scripture written for the trader who never had one.

II.

K-Line Replay

复盘训练

Bar-by-bar replay on real historical markets, names hidden, dates removed. Decisions in slow motion, weighted toward the gate you are working through. The chart becomes a dojo.

III.

Observatory Coach

观心教练

Tell it what happened. It identifies the thief. It routes you to the precise training. Not a conversation — a diagnosis. The next section shows what we mean.
